Mother Mary Campbell Van Horne
Father Walter Fletcher Murray

Background

Grace was the eldest of 3 children and as a kid was a very curious as far as dismantling several alarm clocks to determine how it functioned. She graduated Vassar in 1928 with a bachelor's degree in maths and physics and earned her masters degree and Ph.D at Yale University. Her dissertaion "New Types of Irreducibility Criteria " was published as well. She taught maths at Vassar in 1931 and promoted to associate professor in 1941 In 1930, Grace Murray married a university lecturer professor Vincent Foster Hopper but they divorced in 1945 but she kept his name.

Technological Contributions

In 1949, she was employed as a senior mathematician and developed UNIVAC(the firdt general purpose computer). Hopper's suggestion of developing a whole new programming in English words was shot down as "[she] couldn't do this because computers didn't understand English." She carried on "It's much easier for most people to write an English statement than it is to use symbols," she explained. "So I decided data processors ought to be able to write their programs in English, and the computers would translate them into machine code." Her ideas were constantly rejected for 3 years. Nevertheless, she publisehed her paper on compilers in 1952 In 1952 Hopper had a running compiler that translated mathematical notation into machine code. "So I decided data processors ought to be able to write their programs in English, and the computers would translate them into machine code. That was the beginning of COBOL, a computer language for data processors. I could say 'Subtract income tax from pay' instead of trying to write that in octal code or using all kinds of symbols. COBOL is the major language used today in data processing." In 1954 Hopper's department released some of the first compiler based programming languages like MATH-MATIC and FLOW-MATIC with her being at the forefront of the launch being promoted to director. In 1959, at Conference on Data Systems Languages(formed from computer experts from industry and government) where Hopper served as a technical consultant, the commitee created a new language COmmon Business-Oriented Language which extended FLOW-MATIC and included IBM COMTRAN ideas. From 1967 to 1977 she served as director of Programming Languages in the Navy and was ranked captain in 1973. She developed validation software for COBOL and its compiler for the entire Navy.

Why is she still relevant today?

Grace Hopper has a left a tremendous legacy, as far being labelled "Grandma Cobol". A software bug is an error in a computer programor system that causes it to produce an incorrect or unexpected result. The term "bug" was invented by Grace on 9th september 1945 when a real bug(a moth) short circuited the MARK II Aiken Relay Calculator in Harvard. She won a numerous awards in her lifetime, such as The Society of Women Engineers award, Naval Reserve medal and 40 honorary degrees from university to name a few. In conclusion, Grace Hopper is the best role model for young girls in computer science as despite the gender inequality that she faced in that time, like the Gender pay gap, she is seen to have made in "a man's world.
