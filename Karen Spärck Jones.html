Full Name Karen Ida Boalth Spräck Jones
Date Of Birth 26 August 1935 Huddersfield, Yorkshire
Date Of Death 4 April 2007 (aged 71) Willingham, Cambridgeshire
Education University of Cambridge
Mother Ida Spärck.
Father Owen Jones

Background

Jones had an ordinary childhood being born to a Norweigen mother and British father. Educated in a grammar school, Jones went on to study history in Cambridge where she met her husband Roger Needham another computer scientist.

Technological Contributions

In 1950s she worked at the Cambridge Language Unit which consisted of natural language processing and information retrieval. She created the concept of inverse document frequency, a technology that underlies most modern search engines In 1982 she became part of the team that developed the Alvey Programme.

Why is she still relevant today?

The concept of inverse document frequency (IDF) weighting in information retrieval, which she introduced in a 1972 paper. IDF is used in most search engines today Jones was the recipient of many awards such as ACL Lifetime Achievement Award, BCS Lovelace Medal, ACM-AAAI Allen Newell Award and more. The University of Huddersfield renamed one of its campus buildings in her honour. Although, natural language processing was not a hot topic in her term, Jones sure did make some waves in this field.
